About Me

For the past three years I have been spending my time repairing my summer home damaged by Hurricane Sandy, volunteering as an educator at the Cradle of Aviation museum and Last Hope Animal Shelter, training for a half iron man triathlon and learning web development.

My qualifications include studies in Capability Maturity Models (CMMI), a Certificate in Project Management for IS/IT Professionals from Villanova, and more than eighteen years of application development, project management and team lead experience.

During my career with Depository Trust and Clearing Corporation (DTCC), I managed staff and IT projects for the largest financial services post-trade infrastructure in the world. DTCC is a $23 trillion holding company for six subsidiaries providing clearance, settlement and IS for $1.7 trillion daily transactions in equity, corporate/municipal debt, government/mortgage-backed securities and emerging market sovereign debt trades in the USA.

Beginning as a Purchasing Agent, I rapidly moved into software development and programming, working as IT Project Leader designing a DB2 database driven Trade Repository application able to process more than fifteen million trade records daily.

As an Area Process Specialist I was a key player in meeting the corporate objective of Level 3 CCMI Certification, providing comprehensive process management and team leadership focused on process transformation, on the implementation-related aspects of work products, team workflow, and project methodology usage and tools utilization.

I was a Team Lead responsible for overseeing the design, build and implementation of mission critical systems.

My current goal is to utilize my expertise in project management, applications development, business analysis and team management to launch a second career as a freelance web developer.
